📋 How Year 2 works — Paper 3 & Paper 4

In Year 1 (AS) you sat Paper 1 (Approaches, Issues & Debates) and Paper 2 (Research Methods). For the full A Level you now add Paper 3 and Paper 4. Both are based on two specialist options — you have chosen Clinical and Health psychology. You answer questions on both options in each paper.

Paper 3Specialist Options: Approaches, Issues & Debates · 1h30 · 60 marks · 25% of A Level
Paper 4Specialist Options: Application & Research Methods · 1h30 · 60 marks · 25% of A Level
PaperStructureWhat it tests
Paper 3 4 questions per option (you do 2 options = 30 marks each). Per option: 3 short-answer questions (14 marks) + one structured essay: part (a) describe 6 marks, part (b) evaluate 10 marks. Knowledge + evaluation of theories, explanations & treatments; applying the issues & debates. Heavy on AO3 evaluation.
Paper 4 Section A: 2 structured questions per option (18 marks each). Section B: one planning question (choose 1 of 4) — plan a study (10 marks) + evaluate the plan (14 marks). Applying knowledge + research methods: linking key studies to methods, and designing/planning an investigation.

⚠️ Topics tested in Paper 3 will be different from those tested in Paper 4 in any given series — so you must know all topics, you can't predict which paper a topic lands in. Paper 3 & 4 must be taken in the same exam series.

📑 The 8 Issues & Debates (A Level) — apply these everywhere for evaluation marks
  • Application to everyday life — how the research helps in the real world.
  • Individual vs situational explanations of behaviour.
  • Nature vs nurture.
  • Use of children in psychological research (ethics/validity).
  • Cultural differences.
  • Reductionism vs holism.
  • Determinism vs free-will.
  • Idiographic vs nomothetic.

In Paper 3 part (b) "evaluate" answers, weaving 2–3 of these in is how you reach the top band.

🔬 New Research Methods & concepts at A Level (build on your AS knowledge)
  • Experiments: randomised controlled trials (RCTs).
  • Questionnaires: postal questionnaires; rating scales (forced/fixed choice).
  • Psychometric tests — describe & evaluate.
  • Hypotheses: null, directional (one-tailed), non-directional (two-tailed).
  • Validity including temporal validity.
  • Paper 4 planning adds (vs AS): aims & hypotheses, sample & sampling, ethics, analysis & descriptive statistics, counterbalancing/random allocation, number of observers.
